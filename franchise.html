The Business Case

Fair chance hiring isn't charity.
It's a competitive advantage.

The data is clear: franchises that hire from underrepresented populations outperform on retention, qualify for significant tax relief, and tap a talent pool that competitors ignore. Here's what that means for your bottom line.

Tax Credits

Up to $9,600

The federal Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC) provides up to $9,600 per eligible hire for employers who hire individuals with felony convictions within a year of release. Fair Rise franchisees are pre-positioned to maximize this credit with every hire.

Source: U.S. Department of Labor, WOTC Program

Retention Rates

40% Lower Turnover

Studies consistently show that fair chance employees — particularly those with structured support like transportation and training — stay significantly longer than average QSR hires. Lower churn means lower recruiting costs and a more skilled, stable team.

Source: SHRM Fair Chance Employer Research, 2023

Untapped Talent Pool

70M+ People

Over 70 million Americans have a criminal record, yet the vast majority are shut out of the labor market. Fair Rise franchises gain access to a highly motivated workforce in cities where labor shortages are a constant challenge for food service operators.

Source: Bureau of Justice Statistics
